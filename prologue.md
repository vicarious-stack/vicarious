# Prologue\n\n**The night air over Kothrud smelled of wet concrete and distant diesel, the monsoon clouds still swollen with promise.** The first heavy drop hit the tin roof of the shared PG room, a sudden percussion that made the old fan whir faster, scattering the stale heat that clung to the walls. Kiran felt the coolness of the water splash against his cheek as it ran down the cracked plaster, the metallic tang of rain mixing with the lingering aroma of chai that had long since gone cold in a chipped ceramic cup.\n\nHe lifted his phone, the screen a dim, pulsing rectangle that seemed to breathe in sync with his own shallow breaths. The notification bar glowed a soft amber, a silent reminder of the screen‑time counter that had been climbing ever since he'd first downloaded the "Social Booster" app three months ago. The number read **0 hours 15 minutes**, a trivial number at that moment, but it was the beginning of a habit that would soon dominate his days.\n\nThe room was cramped, the walls lined with faded posters of cricket legends and motivational quotes in Marathi, their colors faded by sun and humidity. A single light bulb flickered above a wooden desk cluttered with textbooks, a battered laptop, and dozens of sticky notes scribbled in Hindi and English – each a fragment of a thought that never quite solidified. The only sound beyond the rain was the low hum of the neighbor's air‑conditioner and the occasional distant honk of an auto‑rickshaw navigating the narrow alleys of Kothrud.\n\nKiran's mind drifted to the **core thesis** that had been whispered to him by a professor in a dusty lecture hall: *"You think you are choosing your life, but your life is choosing you. Every scroll, every comparison, every avoidance, every desire – there is a mechanism. Understanding the mechanism is the first step to freedom.*" He remembered the professor's voice, crisp and low, as if each word carried weight more than the air it displaced. The idea settled in his gut like a stone, dense and unmovable.\n\nHe opened a new tab in his browser, the cursor flickering like a heartbeat. The first link displayed a reel of a lone traveler trekking across the stark, icy valleys of Ladakh, the camera panning over snow‑capped peaks that seemed to pierce the sky. The wind howled in the background, and the traveler’s breath formed clouds of vapor that dissipated almost instantly – a visual reminder of impermanence. Kiran's attention was caught, his eyes widening as the scene shifted to a close‑up of a cracked, weather‑worn boot stepping onto a frozen river. The sound of the crunch was unnervingly crisp, echoing in his small room.\n\nHe swiped left, and the reel changed to a rapid‑fire tutorial on setting up a local development environment for Python, lines of code scrolling past like a waterfall of neon text. Beneath the tutorial, a comment section glowed with emojis and short, encouraging phrases: "Great!", "👍", "🚀", each a tiny dopamine spike that nudged his brain toward a fleeting sense of accomplishment. The scent of the rain intensified, the humidity seeping through the window frame and making the paper notes on his desk feel damp.\n\nAnother swipe revealed a cricket highlight – a six hit by a local hero at the Wankhede Stadium, the crowd roaring, the stadium lights blazing against a night sky that seemed to pulse with collective excitement. The sound of the bat striking the ball reverberated through the speakers, a short, sharp crack that resonated in Kiran's chest. He could almost feel the vibration through the wooden floorboards, the thrum of adrenaline that surged in fans watching from every corner of Pune.\n\nEach image, each sound, each burst of color was a **stimulus** that his brain catalogued, not as a narrative, but as a **prediction error** – a moment where expectation and reality diverged, prompting dopamine to surge. The **digital ledger** on his desk, a small leather‑bound notebook, waited for him to record the incident: the time, the feeling, the intensity of the urge. He hesitated only a moment before opening it, the pages thick and slightly rough to the touch, the smell of aged paper rising as he turned to a fresh leaf.\n\n```json\n{\n  "screen_time": 15,\n  "unlocks": 0,\n  "focus": 0,\n  "code": 0\n}\n```\n\nHe wrote: *"15 min – scrolling through travel, code tutorial, cricket. Physical sensations: cool rain, humid air, faint smell of chai, faint ache in my neck from the laptop angle. Mental: fleeting curiosity, slight excitement, quick dopamine spikes, then a dulling. Noted the first external stimulus of the day.*"\n\nThe rain intensified, a steady drumbeat now, and Kiran felt a growing tension in his shoulders, the weight of the day's **unconscious decisions** pressing down like the dark clouds outside. He glanced at the clock – **7 a.m.** – and realized the night had slipped away while he was caught in the **scroll**, a modern **vicarious** experience that mirrored the ancient practice of watching others' stories unfold from a distance.\n\nHe closed his laptop with a soft click, the sound resonating like a final note in a song that never quite resolved. The room fell silent, save for the rain's rhythm and the faint buzzing of the ceiling fan. Kiran stared at the notebook, the ink drying as if it might capture the moment forever. In that stillness, the **mechanism** of his own attention became unmistakably clear – it was a loop of stimulus, prediction, and reward, a cycle he could now begin to observe, and perhaps, eventually, to **choose** differently.\n\n*Thus began the journey, not as a quest toward a distant summit, but as a series of small, deliberate choices made in the quiet spaces between the scrolls of a screen, between the drops of a monsoon, and within the rooms of a shared life.*
